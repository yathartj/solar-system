STAR OF OUR SOLAR SYSTEM

Sun: "Hey there! I’m the Sun, the center of this whole show.
I’m a giant ball of glowing gas, mostly hydrogen and helium, and I provide the light and heat that make life possible on the planets around me.
Without me, this solar system would be a cold, dark place.

PLANETS

Mercury: "Hi, I’m Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun.
I’m tiny and a bit of a hot mess with extreme temperature changes—hot days and freezing nights.
I’ve got a rocky surface and lots of craters, thanks to not having much of an atmosphere to protect me.

Venus: "Greetings! I’m Venus, the second planet from the Sun.
I’ve got a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making me the hottest planet in the solar system.
My surface is covered in volcanoes and I’ve got some seriously crushing pressure.

Earth: "Hello! I’m Earth, the third planet from the Sun and the only one known to support life.
I’ve got a perfect mix of air, water, and land.
My diverse climates and ecosystems make me quite the vibrant home for all sorts of creatures.

Mars: "Hey! I’m Mars, the fourth planet and often called the Red Planet because of my iron oxide dust.
I’ve got the tallest volcano and the deepest canyon in the solar system.
I’m a bit chilly, but there’s a lot of interest in my past and potential for future exploration.

Jupiter: "Greetings, I’m Jupiter, the largest planet around here.
I’m a gas giant with a massive storm system known as the Great Red Spot.
I’ve got a bunch of moons and a powerful magnetic field.
I’m like the heavyweight champion of this solar system.

Saturn: "Hi there! I’m Saturn, famous for my spectacular rings.
I’m a gas giant like Jupiter, but with a more elegant look thanks to my rings made of ice and rock particles.
I also have a bunch of moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere.

Uranus: "Hello! I’m Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun.
I’m unique because I rotate on my side, which gives me extreme seasonal variations.
I’m an ice giant with a cold, windy atmosphere and a faint ring system.

Neptune: "Hey, I’m Neptune, the eighth planet and the farthest from the Sun. I’m known for my deep blue color and strong winds.
My atmosphere has dark storms, and I’m another ice giant with a system of rings and a few moons.

DWARF PLANETS

Pluto: Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto is now classified as a dwarf planet.
It has a complex surface with mountains, plains, and a large heart-shaped glacier.

Eris: Located in the scattered disk, Eris is slightly smaller than Pluto but more massive.
It has a highly eccentric orbit and is known for its bright, icy surface.

Haumea: Found in the Kuiper Belt, Haumea is notable for its elongated shape due to its rapid rotation.
It has two known moons and a ring system.

Makemake: Another resident of the Kuiper Belt, Makemake is one of the largest known dwarf planets.
It has a surface covered with methane ice.
